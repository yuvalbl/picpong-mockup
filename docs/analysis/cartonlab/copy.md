# Cartonlab — Copy & Messaging Analysis

Reference site for **Picpong** (printing house for conference / event / exhibition products).
Scope of this document: **copy / text / messaging only** — voice, headlines, value props, CTAs, microcopy.
Source: https://cartonlab.com/en (English locale). Analyzed 2026-05-31.

Cartonlab is a Spanish (Murcia-based, Madrid-rooted, Miami-present) studio of architects/designers
that designs and manufactures **cardboard furniture, stands, displays and ephemeral architecture**
for brands and events. Their whole positioning rides on one tension: *temporary, recyclable
cardboard → extraordinary, high-impact brand spaces.* That tension is the engine of all their copy.

## 1. Brand voice & tone

A blend of **confident**, **craft-proud**, **eco-led**, and quietly **playful** — written by
designers who take the work seriously but not themselves.

| Trait | Evidence (verbatim) |
|---|---|
| **Confident / bold** | "Stand out for real" · "Built to get noticed" · "Get recognized and remembered." |
| **Magnetic / showy ("wow")** | "Design to amaze. Build to disappear." · "Looks like magic. It's Cartonlab." · "Efecto wow y resultado de alta calidad" |
| **Eco-led, but value-first** | "for brands that impact without a trace." · "no stock, no waste, and optimizing every resource." · "FSC-certified, 100% recyclable materials, reusable systems." |
| **Craft-proud / expert** | "We are a team of architects and designers…" · "More than 12 years of experience designing with cardboard." · "We select the right type of cardboard for each project based on its use and demands." |
| **Warm / human / playful** | "Real people, cardboard obsessed. Pleased to meet you." · "Keep Imagining" (sign-off) · email aliases "bonjour@", "hello@" |
| **Reassuring / frictionless** | "Everything optimized so your project moves forward without friction." · "We design it. You build it. Easy as that." · "Faster, less hassle" |
| **Philosophical / aphoristic** | "Great ideas don't need to last forever." · "Temporary is Extraordinary." · "the temporary deserves to be extraordinary." |

**Signature rhetorical device:** short, paradoxical two-part lines that pair *ephemerality* with
*impact*. This is the most repeated and most "ownable" stylistic move in the whole site:
- "Design to amaze. Build to disappear."
- "Great ideas don't need to last forever. Temporary can be extraordinary."
- "We design it. You build it. Easy as that."

**Language register:** Plain, punchy English, short sentences, lots of imperatives. Spanish origin
shows through in a few un-localized phrases ("bussines"/"commerces" typos, Spanish benefit bullets
left untranslated: "Sencillo de montar", "Fácil de transportar", "Resistente y funcional"). For
Picpong we'd want the *energy* of this voice without the localization slips.

## 3. Value propositions / key selling points (repeated across site)

These themes recur on the homepage, services, category, and product pages:

1. **High visual impact / "wow"** — "Stand out for real", "Built to get noticed", "Design to amaze",
   "Efecto wow", "stunning", "Looks like magic."
2. **Sustainability without compromise** — "impact without a trace", "100% recyclable and reusable",
   "FSC-certified", "no stock, no waste", "certified materials of low environmental impact."
3. **Temporary by design** — "Great ideas don't need to last forever", "Temporary is Extraordinary",
   "Build to disappear", "ephemeral architecture."
4. **Lightweight + strong (the cardboard paradox)** — "lightweight, robust and easy to assemble",
   "weighs nothing and supports up to 250 kg", "Combines lightness and resistance",
   "Resistance: Up to 320 lb."
5. **Easy / fast / frictionless** — "ready to order in one click", "Assembly time: Less than 5 minutes",
   "We design it. You build it. Easy as that.", "Faster, less hassle", "without friction."
6. **Full-service, end-to-end** — "Design, production, shipping, and assembly." ·
   "We'll take it from sketch to reality on time and on budget."
7. **Made-to-order / exclusive** — "we design and manufacture on demand, with no stock" ·
   "exclusive designs that you will only find on the Cartonlab website."
8. **Brandable / customizable** — "Integrate your graphics into the furniture. Stronger visual impact
   and a consistent brand identity."
9. **Two ways to buy** — productized shop ("one click") vs. bespoke projects ("Tailored solutions for
   unique ideas"). This dual model is front-and-center on the homepage.
10. **Expert credibility** — "team of architects and designers", "More than 12 years of experience."

## 4. Product-page copy patterns

Product pages follow a consistent, scannable template. Order of copy blocks:

1. **Product name + one-line use framing**
   - e.g. Taray chair: "Cardboard chair" → "Full of possibilities at your bussines, commerces and events"
   - This "Full of possibilities at your bussines, commerces and events" line is reused verbatim
     across multiple products (Taray chair, Taray table, AZERO) — a templated tagline slot.

2. **Spec block (imperial units, US-facing)** — terse `Label: value` list:
   - Taray chair: "Sizes: 30″Height x 18″Width x 22″Depth" · "Weight: 5,5 lb" ·
     "Brown Kraft Style. (Contact us to know about other options and their prices)" ·
     "Assembly time: Less than 5 minutes" · "Resistance: Up to 320 lb."
   - Taray table: "Weights: Between 11 lb and 17 lb depending on the model" ·
     "Resistance: 66-220 lb depending on the model" · "Assembly time: Less than 10 minutes"

3. **Materials + manufacturing + eco claim (bundled together)**:
   - "Designed and produced in Spain and USA with FSC certificated materials"
   - "Manufactured with the very resistant 4 waves corrugated cardboard, Kraft style"
   - Table variant: "Manufactured with high resistance 0.28″ cuadruplex corrugated cardboard in Kraft finish"
   - "100% recyclable and reusable" (appears on every product)

4. **Benefit bullets (4 fixed icons/phrases, left in Spanish)**:
   - "Sencillo de montar" (Simple to assemble)
   - "Fácil de transportar" (Easy to transport)
   - "Resistente y funcional" (Resistant and functional)
   - "Efecto wow y resultado de alta calidad" (WOW effect, high-quality result)

5. **Customization upsell block** (identical across products):
   - Header: "CUSTOMIZABLE PRODUCTS" / "Your business, Your style"
   - Body: "Integrate your graphics into the furniture. Stronger visual impact and a consistent brand
     identity throughout your project"

6. **Print/order how-it-works (on printable products, e.g. AZERO)** — numbered 5-step list:
   1. "Choose the 'Print' option"
   2. "Add to your cart"
   3. "Receive your Templates"
   4. "Send us your artwork"
   5. "Receive your order"

7. **CTAs:** "Add to cart", "Contact us", "Online Shop", "Continue Shopping", and a locator CTA
   ("Find Taray").

**Framing takeaways for Picpong:** specs are stated as plain `Label: value` lines (dimensions,
weight, **resistance/load**, **assembly time**); benefits are framed around the buyer's *event/brand*
("at your business and events"), not the object; sustainability is woven into the spec block rather
than siloed; and customization (print your graphics) is a consistent upsell on every product.

## 7. Sustainability / eco messaging (how they frame cardboard)

Cardboard is *both* the material constraint and the brand's whole story. They reframe its
"temporariness" as a virtue rather than a limitation.

**Core eco lines (verbatim):**
- "for brands that impact without a trace."
- "no stock, no waste, and optimizing every resource."
- "FSC-certified, 100% recyclable materials, reusable systems."
- "100% recyclable and reusable" (on every product)
- "manufactured with certified materials of low environmental impact"
- "All our materials are FSC-certified, guaranteeing responsible sourcing."
- "We reduce the environmental footprint of your design by making it 100% recyclable and reusable.
   Our products are made from certified sustainable materials." (Services → Products)
- "Solutions produced in cardboard for environmentally friendly events." (Event Solutions category)
- "An organic and natural material, ideal for creating cozy spaces with an eye-catching appearance"
   (Kor Water case study)
- "Made from 100% recyclable cardboard"

**How "temporary cardboard" is reframed as a positive:**
- "Great ideas don't need to last forever." / "Temporary is Extraordinary."
- "Design to amaze. Build to disappear."
- Ephemerality + recyclability are presented as the *point*, not a trade-off — events are temporary,
   so a recyclable, reusable, waste-free material is the *right* choice, not a compromise.

**Material credibility framing (from cardboard-types article):**
- Cardboard positioned as "resistant, light, economical and very versatile".
- Honeycomb cardboard: "great rigidity with the lowest possible weight."
- Performance proof: "weighs nothing and supports up to 250 kg" / "Combines lightness and resistance."
- "We select the right type of cardboard for each project based on its use and demands: corrugated,
   inverted corrugated, honeycomb, or solid board."

**Strategic note for Picpong:** Cartonlab leads with **impact and design** and uses sustainability as
a *supporting* proof point ("impact without a trace" pairs both in one line), rather than leading
with guilt or eco-credentials. FSC + "100% recyclable & reusable" + "no waste / made-to-order" form
their three-legged sustainability stool. The made-to-order / no-stock model is itself framed as an
eco-virtue (no overproduction).

The About page humanizes the studio: architects who are "obsessed" with cardboard, with a dated
origin timeline.

**Lead (verbatim):**
> "Real people, cardboard obsessed. Pleased to meet you."

**Mission (verbatim):**
> "We are a team of architects and designers who believe the temporary deserves to be extraordinary.
> That great ideas don't need permanent materials."

**Operating philosophy (verbatim):**
> "That's why we design and manufacture on demand, with no stock, no waste, and optimizing every
> resource. Cardboard spaces and furniture for businesses and events. Spanish-born. World-ready."

**Origin timeline (verbatim):**
- *1999:* "Founders Carlos Abadía Suanzes-Carpegna, Nacho Bautista, and Pablo García Mora meet in the
  late 90s at ETSAM (Madrid). The starting point of Cartonlab and a way of working that continues to
  this day."
- *2006:* "Together with Ernesto Sierra, we find Moho Arquitectos in Murcia. The beginning of a
  multidisciplinary practice spanning architecture, urban planning, product design, exhibitions, and
  publications."
- *2009:* "We find Cartonlab within the DA (Artistic District) program as an experimental initiative
  exploring cardboard and its possibilities in design and ephemeral architecture."

**Narrative shape:** human/warm hook → belief/mission → operating model (on-demand, zero-waste) →
credibility via founders + dated milestones. The story explicitly connects the *founders' architecture
background* to the *quality/credibility* of the product, and positions cardboard as a deliberate
design philosophy, not a budget choice. CTA on the page: **"Get in touch."**

## Implications for Picpong (quick synthesis)

1. **Own a paradox line.** Cartonlab's whole voice hangs on "temporary → extraordinary" /
   "amaze → disappear". Picpong should find its own ownable two-part paradox (e.g. impact vs.
   ephemerality, or print vs. waste).
2. **Lead with impact, support with eco.** Sustainability is a proof point ("impact without a trace"),
   never the opening guilt-trip.
3. **Dual CTA lanes.** Productized "shop now / one click" + bespoke "tell us about your project."
4. **Spec template:** `Dimensions · Weight · Resistance/Load · Assembly time · Material (certified) ·
   100% recyclable` — terse, scannable, buyer-event-framed benefits.
5. **Customization as a standing upsell** on every product ("Integrate your graphics… consistent
   brand identity").
6. **Human, expert origin story** — architects/designers + dated milestones build credibility.
7. **A memorable sign-off** like "Keep Imagining" reused site-wide.
8. **Localize fully** — avoid the un-translated Spanish bullets/filters and typos that weaken
   Cartonlab's otherwise strong English voice.

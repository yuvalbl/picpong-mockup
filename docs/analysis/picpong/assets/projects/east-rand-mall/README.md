## What the photos show (vision analysis)

- **Represents:** A custom cardboard "face-in-hole" selfie/photo-wall built by Picpong to celebrate East Rand Mall's 20th birthday. The three photos show the same free-standing double-sided board photographed from different angles inside the Picpong factory: one face (Pic_3) carries the "20 - My Kind of Place Since 1992" anniversary logo, and the other face (Pic_4, Pic_5) is the "Happy 20th birthday, East Rand Mall!" panel with three party-hat figures whose heads are die-cut as holes so people can poke their faces through for photos.
- **Structures / X-Board:** A single tall, free-standing self-supporting cardboard photo/selfie wall (face-cutout totem), roughly 1.8-2 m high. It is built from flat printed board panels joined at a right-angle: Pic_5 clearly shows an L/T-shaped configuration with a plain white panel acting as a perpendicular support fin/stabilizer behind the printed front face, letting it stand upright without external bracing. The board's cut top edge and laminated panel thickness are visible. The "people" side has three circular die-cut head holes (one shows the warehouse visible straight through it) for face-in-hole photo interaction. The whole unit sits on a flat white floor plinth/base board for display and photography.
- **Branding:** East Rand Mall 20th-anniversary branding. The anniversary logo is a gold/bronze 3D "20" over a stylised red-and-grey arch/sunburst crest with the tagline strapline "MY KIND OF PLACE SINCE 1992". The party-graphics face reads "Happy 20th birthday, East Rand Mall!" in white type. Strong red brand background throughout, topped with a colourful multi-coloured balloon border (red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple) and white line-drawn party figures with party hats, a birthday cake with candles, confetti and a cocktail glass.
- **Setting:** indoor
- **Reuse:** Good thumbnail / portfolio candidate, not a hero. The product (a polished, fully-printed double-sided face-cutout selfie wall) is appealing and shows Picpong's print quality and self-standing cardboard engineering well, but the backdrop is a cluttered factory floor with pallets, exit signs and a worker, which reads as "behind the scenes" rather than a finished event installation. Best use: a tight cropped thumbnail focused on the board itself (Pic_4 is the sharpest, most front-on and best-lit frame), or a "how it's made / our workshop" supporting image.
